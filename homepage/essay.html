Meaning

My journey

When people think of the Overseas Chinese Mission Church, they think it is a regular church full of Chinese people. It is in the heart of New York City Chinatown. Everybody in the church is Chinese. We all love the same food, which is cha su and rice. They are a full community together.

However, I didn’t feel connected with this church. I was figuring out why people love the Overseas Chinese Mission Church. I never understood that. I was in a room full of Chinese kids who were in the same grade as me, but I never knew how to interact with them. I was as shy as a squirrel. I wanted to talk with other people, but my soul was dark. It was betraying me to talk with other people. It was soon about to change.

Fortunately, I established different friendships in my church. At the same time, there were verses in the Bible that inspired me for the rest of my life. A leader at my church named Jon talked about how God changed his life. There were questions that people wanted to ask him about Christianity. I asked him what should I do to get closer with God, and why did you get baptized. He answered all of those questions like a scholar with ease. One of his answers was that people should pray. The word prayer is such a strong word because that is how people in my church got through difficult times. Also, Jon advised that the youth should listen to different Christian songs. Once I listened to the different Christianity songs, it opened a new portal for me. There were Christian songs that touched me deeply in my heart, like Awesome God, My Living Hope, and Gratitude. Surprisingly, those songs and prayers comforted me like a baby. After three years of praying and listening to Gospel music, I wanted to get baptized.

I got baptized on August 31st 2025. That day was the best day of my life because I got to see everybody that I love, like my church friends, family members, and family friends to see me. That event should have been essential, but it wasn’t. It was in the middle of the night. Whenever somebody gets baptized, he/she would need to share their testimony to their whole entire youth group. Before my testimony, there was Christian music that was happening in a fieldhouse where my youth group and I were in. After ten minutes of listening to the Christian music, I started to sob. I felt isolated. I needed to go outside because I didn’t want my youth to see me cry. I didn’t know why I was crying. I believe that it was the Holy Spirit who was with me. As I am writing my story, God knew what I was going through. The pain that I had endured for such a long time of being alone and the demons that I had. After I finished crying, God gave me the strength that I needed to finish my journey. It was something that I didn’t expect.

I walked back into church. As one of the students who finished up their testimony, my youth director pulled me aside and told me that I needed to speak from my heart for my testimony. Once that student finished her testimony, I took one more deep breath and I walked on stage for my testimony. Everybody was clapping for me. I was speechless because all of the youth were clapping. I was still admiring, just looking at all of the people who were here. And for some reason, they just started clapping for me again. At this point in time, I was smiling because look at how far I have become. When they finished clapping again, I spoke from the heart. I told them every single detail possible on how I was suffering back then but God healed me spiritually and mentally. I told them I was grateful that they were there for me in my darkest times. Those were the things that kept to myself because I didn’t have the courage to say it to my church. They didn’t even know that I was suffering back then until that moment. As I was telling them my testimony, I felt like I was at peace. The huge weight on my body that was holding me back was withering away. However, my legs felt weak but I knew that I needed to be strong and confident so that my message would be done. When I was finished with my testimony, I knew that my work was done and there was an applause.

Suddenly, there were people in my youth who wanted to share something personal. The things that they shared out were personal stories that they have experienced with the dark struggles that they were encountering. Fortunately, they said that God was there for them in their darkened times. I was amazed that they were sharing these topics even though they had a choice to not share it out. After the stories were over, a ton of people were crying and praying. There were junior and senior girls who said that they were emotional after hearing my story and started to cry. I was stunned that they were crying when they were hearing my story. At this point, I knew that God was there. This event was the deciding factor that I finally found my purpose in my life. I want to become a loving Christian. Overseas Chinese Mission Church is like my second home. I realized that sometimes the things we don’t like or understand end up shaping us on who we are. To the people who are in the Overseas Chinese Mission Church, we are a strong community together when we are united. We represent the Christians that are in New York City Chinatown. Surely, my story resonates with my beloved church.
